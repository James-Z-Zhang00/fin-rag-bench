# SEC Filing Types Guide

## 10-K (Annual Report)
The 10-K is a comprehensive annual report filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). Required for all publicly traded companies in the United States.

**Filing Deadline:** Within 60-90 days after fiscal year end (depending on company size)

**Key Sections:**
- Part I: Business overview, risk factors, properties
- Part II: Financial data, management discussion & analysis (MD&A)
- Part III: Management information, executive compensation
- Part IV: Financial statements and exhibits

**Use Cases:** Long-term investment analysis, understanding business model, assessing risks

## 10-Q (Quarterly Report)
Quarterly report providing updates on financial performance and operations.

**Filing Deadline:** 40-45 days after quarter end

**Content:** 
- Condensed financial statements (unaudited)
- MD&A for the quarter
- Updates on material events
- Changes in risk factors

**Frequency:** Filed three times per year (Q1, Q2, Q3). Q4 results are included in annual 10-K.

## 8-K (Current Report)
Used to report material events or corporate changes that shareholders should know about.

**Filing Deadline:** Within 4 business days of the event

**Common Triggers:**
- Merger or acquisition announcements
- CEO or CFO departure
- Bankruptcy filing
- Major contract wins or losses
- Changes in auditor
- Earnings announcements

## Relationship Between Filings:
10-K provides comprehensive annual view. 10-Q updates quarterly progress. 8-K reports significant events between quarters. Together, they provide complete picture of company's status and performance.

## Financial Statement Components:
All major filings include:
1. Balance Sheet - Assets, liabilities, equity at point in time
2. Income Statement - Revenue, expenses, profit over period
3. Cash Flow Statement - Operating, investing, financing cash flows
4. Statement of Shareholders' Equity - Changes in equity accounts

## Investor Strategy:
Read 10-K annually for deep understanding. Monitor 10-Qs for quarterly trends. Watch 8-Ks for material changes that might affect investment thesis.
