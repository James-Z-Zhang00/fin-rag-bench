Financial Investment Glossary

Key Financial Metrics:

Revenue (Sales):
Total income generated from normal business operations. Calculated by multiplying price by quantity sold. Also called "top line" because it appears at the top of income statements.

Net Income (Profit):
Revenue minus all expenses, taxes, and costs. Also called "bottom line" or "earnings." Key profitability metric for investors.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):
Net income divided by total shares outstanding. Indicates how much profit is allocated to each share. Higher EPS generally indicates better profitability.

Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio):
Stock price divided by earnings per share. Common valuation metric. Average market P/E is around 15-20. Higher P/E may indicate growth expectations or overvaluation.

Market Capitalization (Market Cap):
Total value of company's outstanding shares. Calculated as share price multiplied by shares outstanding. Categories:
- Large Cap: >$10 billion
- Mid Cap: $2-10 billion
- Small Cap: $300 million - $2 billion

Free Cash Flow (FCF):
Cash from operations minus capital expenditures. Represents cash available for dividends, debt repayment, or reinvestment. More reliable than net income.

Gross Margin:
(Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue. Expressed as percentage. Indicates pricing power and efficiency. Software companies typically have 70-90% gross margins.

Operating Margin:
Operating income divided by revenue. Shows profitability from core operations before interest and taxes. Higher margins indicate better operational efficiency.

Return on Equity (ROE):
Net income divided by shareholder equity. Measures how efficiently company uses shareholder capital. ROE above 15% is generally considered good.

Debt-to-Equity Ratio:
Total liabilities divided by shareholder equity. Indicates financial leverage. Ratio above 2.0 may indicate high financial risk.

Year-over-Year (YoY):
Comparison method that compares current period to same period in previous year. Eliminates seasonal variations. Example: Q4 2023 vs. Q4 2022.

Analyst Consensus:
Average of analyst estimates for metrics like EPS or revenue. Companies "beat" expectations when actual results exceed consensus.

Guidance:
Forward-looking statements from management about expected future performance. Typically provided quarterly or annually.
